title: Philanthropy
---

AI-generated wealth could create $37-100B/year in new philanthropic capital — a "third wave" after Carnegie/Rockefeller (industrial) and Gates/Buffett (internet). Bottleneck isn't capital, it's a shortage of capable founders/allocators to deploy it toward civilization-scale problems (AI safety, etc.) — needs "a Silicon Valley for public goods." [3rd Wave of Philanthropy](https://nanransohoff.substack.com/p/the-third-wave-of-american-philanthropy)

Many global problems lack a single accountable owner ("orphaned problems") — proposes funding "General Managers": founder-type leaders given real autonomy, competitive pay, and long-term backing, the way smallpox eradication or marriage equality succeeded under a visionary leader, not a committee. [General Managers for World Problems](https://nanransohoff.substack.com/p/there-should-be-general-managers)

Atlas of Innovation: a toolkit matching funding mechanism (grants, advance market commitments, etc.) to the type of ambitious R&D goal — funding approach should match the mission. [UI Experiment: Atlas of Innovation](https://atlasofinnovation.org/)

OpenAI Foundation's People-First AI Fund: a concrete instance of AI-era philanthropic capital — grants to community nonprofits exploring how AI can strengthen their work. [Economic Futures in the Age of AI](https://openaifoundation.smapply.us/prog/people-first-ai-fund/)
