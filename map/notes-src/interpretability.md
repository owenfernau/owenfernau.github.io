---
title: Interpretability
---

Concepts in neural networks often organize as curved manifolds in activation space, not linear directions — steering along the fitted manifold gives precise control, while linear steering vectors produce incoherent outputs. Manifolds reportedly give cleaner, more unified representations than sparse autoencoders (SAEs), which tend to fragment concepts into disconnected pieces.
