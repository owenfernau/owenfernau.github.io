AI-generated wealth could create $37-100B/year in new philanthropic capital — a "third wave" after Carnegie/Rockefeller (industrial) and Gates/Buffett (internet). Bottleneck isn't capital, it's a shortage of capable founders/allocators to deploy it toward civilization-scale problems (AI safety, etc.) — needs "a Silicon Valley for public goods." 3rd Wave of Philanthropy

Many global problems lack a single accountable owner ("orphaned problems") — proposes funding "General Managers": founder-type leaders given real autonomy, competitive pay, and long-term backing, the way smallpox eradication or marriage equality succeeded under a visionary leader, not a committee. General Managers for World Problems

Atlas of Innovation: a toolkit matching funding mechanism (grants, advance market commitments, etc.) to the type of ambitious R&D goal — funding approach should match the mission. UI Experiment: Atlas of Innovation

OpenAI Foundation's People-First AI Fund: a concrete instance of AI-era philanthropic capital — grants to community nonprofits exploring how AI can strengthen their work. Economic Futures in the Age of AI
